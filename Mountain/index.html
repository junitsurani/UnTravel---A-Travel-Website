A jungle is a dense and lush ecosystem typically found in tropical regions, characterized by a high diversity of plant and animal species. It consists of thick vegetation, including tall trees, vines, and various plant species, creating a complex and interconnected habitat. Jungles play a crucial role in maintaining global biodiversity and are often referred to as rainforests when located in regions with consistent rainfall. These ecosystems are home to a wide array of wildlife, from insects and amphibians to mammals and birds, making them vital for the overall health of the planet.
